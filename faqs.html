Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my garage door opener reverse back up instead of closing?

Your Garage door opener may reverse back up for a number of reasons:

1. If you have P.E.Beams (photo Electric Beams – a device normally black in colour and found mounted at bumper height in the door opening) fitted it could mean that there could have been an obstruction whilst the door was trying to close, or the P.E.Beam could be dirty so as a safety precaution the door opener has either stopped or reopened to avoid any injury or damage being caused to the object.

2. Your door may have hit something or jammed momentarily and has opened in an attempt to avoid damage.

3. The door has finished closing and has reversed back up.This suggests that the limits need adjustment or that the gears are worn or broken.

Why is my manual Garage Door so heavy?

Garage doors are one of, if not the largest moving object in your home and can weight sometimes in excess of 200kg…suprised? Well you might be as they don’t seem that heavy to lift as the doors are usually counter balanced by springs. If the springs are not set up correctly however, you may find the door very heavy to lift. Another possibility is that the springs may need readjusting as a result of some tension being lost through time or one or both of the springs may have snapped, which would be be visibly noticeable. If any of these relate to you it may be a good idea to have it checked out.

My automatic Garage Door won’t open or is stuck in the close position?

Your automatic Garage Door could be stuck in the close position or just won’t open for a number of reasons.

1. The counter balance spring could be broken and is overloading the system or it has lost tension and needs to be retentioned.

2. The door could be jammed somewhere or maybe the locking mechanism has accidentally become engaged somehow.

3. There could be a fault with the electronics either the control board or the remotes, try replacing batteries.
