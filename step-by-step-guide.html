Step-by-step Guides

Disengaging your Roller Door Motor

Step 1- Identify the (usually) red disengagement cord that hangs down from your motor. The motor displayed shows what a standard disengagement cord looks like.

Step 2- Pull down on the disengagement cord and release. Depending on the type of roller door motor you have you may or may not hear a click.

Step 3- Pull garage door up or down, it should now move freely. If this method does not work you may not have pulled down on the cord hard enough or your clutch assembly may be jammed or broken. If this is the case call a garage door company to try and get it repaired.

Disengaging your Tilt or Panel Door

Step 1. Locate the (usually) red disengagement cord that hangs down from the “motor shuttle”. The image displayed is a generic shuttle, yours may look similar to it. You will notice the clip the rope is tied to is pointing horizontally.

Step 2. Pull down on the disengagement cord so the clip it is tied to faces down. This will indicate the motor is disengaged.

Step 3. Pull the door up or down, it should now be disengaged and move freely. If you can not find the disengagement cord it has most likely fallen off. If you get a pair of pliers you should be able to pull the clip down into the disengaged position if needed.

Re-engaging your Roller Door Motor

Step 1- Identify the (usually) red disengagement cord that hangs down from your motor. The photo displayed shows the disengagement cord, your motor will look something similar.

Step 2- Pull down on the disengagement cord and release. Depending on the type of roller door motor you have you may or may not hear a click.

Step 3- You should now be able to use your remote control to operate the door.

If you can’t find the disengagement cord it has probably fallen off. If this does not fix the issue you may need further motor repairs.

If you have a Merlin 430R system, this method will not work for you. You will need to manually push up the red lever that the string is connected to in order to engage the motor as this clutch assembly is not spring loaded.

Re-engaging your Tilt / Panel Door Motor

Step 1- Locate the (usually) red disengagement cord that hangs down from the “motor shuttle”. The image displayed is a generic shuttle, yours may look similar to it. You will notice the clip the rope is tied to is pointing down.

Step 2- Grab hold of the disengagement cord and pull up and away from the door. You should see the clip snap into a horizontal position.

Step 3- Press your remote control and let it run. This should click the shuttle and index back in and allow the door to run.
